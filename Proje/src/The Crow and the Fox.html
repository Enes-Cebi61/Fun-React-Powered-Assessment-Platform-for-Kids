The Crow and the Fox

Once upon a time, in the depths of the forest, there lived a clever bird named Crow. The Crow had gained fame as the smartest and quickest thinker in the forest. One day, he encountered a cunning creature named Fox. Upon seeing the Crow's shiny and beautiful feathers, the Fox was impressed. The Fox approached the Crow and said, "Dear Crow, I am so proud of your beautiful feathers. I would like to see them up close. Could you show me one of your feathers?" The Crow fell for the Fox's flattery and wanted to show him a feather. He plucked a feather and handed it to the Fox. After taking the feather, the Fox thanked the Crow and swiftly ran into the depths of the forest. A few days later, at the forest meeting attended by various birds, they shared that the Fox had stolen the Crow's feather and deceived many birds using it. The Crow felt embarrassed and immediately set out to find the Fox. When he found the Fox, the Fox still looked at the Crow with a falsely innocent expression. The Crow asked for his feather back. Instead of returning the feather, the Fox laughed and slowly walked away. The Crow realized he had fallen for the Fox's deception. From that day on, the Crow decided to be more cautious and think twice before trusting others.
